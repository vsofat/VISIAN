Meet the Team

Vishwaa Sofat, Presidential Candidate

Hi Stuy!

My name is Vishwaa Sofat and I am the presidential candidate for the VISIAN campaign. My high school experience has been defined by my participation in the Student Union and other leadership forums.

Over the last three years, I have devoted myself to creating key changes at Stuyvesant but also beyond our school. Being a part of Student Union as Freshman Caucus President, Sophomore Caucus President and currently serving as Student Union Vice President and student representative on the SLT has allowed me to not only grow as a person and a leader but also pursue key changes. I have also had the opportunity to make an impact outside of Stuyvesant through my involvement in the Borough Student Advisory Council and Congresswoman Maloney’s Congressional Youth Cabinet, as well as my work with the Indian Consulate.

When I’m not writing policy proposals or sending emails, you can find catching up on Hindi and Punjabi movies or catching up on on my CS homework (sorry Mr. K). I am also involved in Model UN, Cricket, and Big Sibs amongst other things. A fun fact about me is that I have lived in 4 different states and 2 different countries — a global citizen, if you would.

I have one last year to pursue and accomplish change and for that reason, I urge you to vote for our ticket. VISIAN is built around each and every one of you. It builds community and carries us all together as we seek reform. It changes the narrative around student leadership making us all equals in the process of achieving new heights. With your vote and support, it’ll soon be clear that 2020 VISIAN was the right choice.

Julian Giordano, Vice Presidential Candidate

Hi, my name is Julian, and I am running for Vice President with the VISIAN campaign. Since my Freshman year, I have led the External Affairs Department of the SU and devoted myself to working with external organizations to improve the lives of all Stuyvesant Students. I currently represent Stuyvesant at the Manhattan Borough Student Advisory Council (BSAC), Chancellor’s Student Advisory Council (CSAC), and at the NYU Metro Center’s Youth Technical Assistance Center for Addressing Disproportionality (YTAC-D). Being a part of the Student Union has been an amazing experience, and I’ve had the opportunity to learn from and work with amazing people while helping to improve Stuyvesant and the student experience.

Outside of the SU, I work on campaigns for State Assembly and City Council, help run my neighborhood’s Block Association, and am a writer and photographer for the Opinions and Photo sections of the Spectator, respectively. In my free time, I love to travel, practice vinyasa yoga, and watch Broadway musicals. A fun fact about me is that I almost exclusively listen to music from the 1970s.

I’m confident that our ticket will be able to bring meaningful change to the lives of every single student at Stuy, and I encourage you to vote for us. Our platform is built on transparency, accountability, and inclusivity, and we hope to form a lasting bridge between all members of the Stuyvesant community that make any achievements possible.
